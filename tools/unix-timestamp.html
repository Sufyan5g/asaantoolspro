What is a Unix Timestamp?

A Unix timestamp (also known as Epoch time) is the number of seconds that have passed since 00:00:00 UTC on 1 January 1970. It's a universal standard used by developers and computer systems to track time without worrying about time zones.

How to Use This Converter?

  • Timestamp to Date: Enter a Unix timestamp (e.g., `1672531200`) into the first box, and its human-readable date will appear in the second.
  • Date to Timestamp: Enter a date and time (e.g., `2023-01-01 10:30:00`) into the second box, and the corresponding Unix timestamp will be generated.
