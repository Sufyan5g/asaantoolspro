What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure that uses your height and weight to work out if your weight is healthy. For most adults, a BMI of 18.5 to 24.9 is in a healthy range. A BMI of 25 to 29.9 is in the overweight range, and a BMI of 30 or more is in the obese range.

Limitations of BMI: Is It Always Accurate?

While BMI is a very useful tool for the general population, it's important to understand its limitations. BMI does not differentiate between fat and muscle. Therefore, for individuals with a high muscle mass, such as athletes or bodybuilders, their BMI might fall into the 'overweight' category even if they have a healthy level of body fat. Similarly, it may not be entirely accurate for older adults who tend to lose muscle mass. Always use BMI as a general guideline and consult a healthcare professional for a complete health assessment.
