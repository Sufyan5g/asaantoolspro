What is EMI and Why Should You Calculate It Before Taking a Loan?

Taking a loan is a big financial decision. One of the most important terms you'll encounter is "EMI," which stands for Equated Monthly Installment. Understanding this is key to managing your finances effectively.

What Exactly is an EMI?

An EMI is the fixed amount of money you pay to the bank or lender every month to repay your loan. Each EMI payment consists of two parts:

  • Principal Amount: The actual amount of money you borrowed.
  • Interest Amount: The cost you pay to the bank for lending you the money.

In the beginning, a larger portion of your EMI goes towards interest. As you continue to pay, more of your EMI starts to clear the principal amount.

Why is it Crucial to Calculate Your EMI in Advance?

Calculating your EMI beforehand helps you understand if you can comfortably afford the monthly payments without straining your budget. It allows you to plan your expenses and avoid financial stress in the future.

How to Calculate Your EMI Easily?

The formula for EMI can be complex. The easiest way is to use an online calculator where you just need to enter the loan amount, interest rate, and loan duration.
