What is a QR Code? A Simple Explanation

You've probably seen them everywhere: on posters, product packaging, and restaurant tables. A QR Code, or "Quick Response" code, is a type of two-dimensional barcode that can store a variety of information. It's essentially a bridge between the physical world and the digital world.

How Do QR Codes Work?

A QR code works in a similar way to a supermarket barcode. Each black square and dot within the grid represents a piece of information. When you scan it with your smartphone's camera, your phone's software decodes these patterns and translates them into a usable format, like a website link, plain text, or contact information.

Where Are QR Codes Used?

Their versatility makes them useful in countless situations:

  • Marketing: Linking to a website or a special offer from a physical poster or flyer.
  • Restaurants: Providing a digital menu to reduce physical contact.
  • Payments: Making quick and easy mobile payments in stores.
  • Information Sharing: Sharing WiFi passwords, contact details (vCards), or event information.
