Never Get Confused by Sale Prices Again: How to Calculate a Discount

We've all been there: you see a sign that says "40% OFF!" and you have to do some quick mental math to figure out the final price. Is it a good deal? How much will you actually save? Calculating discounts is a practical skill that can save you money and help you shop smarter.

In this guide, we'll show you the simple formula to calculate any discount in seconds.

The Simple Discount Formula

There are two main steps to finding the final price of a discounted item.

Step 1: Calculate the Saved Amount

First, figure out the actual monetary value of the discount.

The Formula: (Original Price × Discount Percentage) / 100

Example: A shirt costs Rs. 2,500 and is on a 30% discount.

  • (2500 × 30) / 100 = 750

So, you will save Rs. 750 on this shirt.

Step 2: Calculate the Final Price

Now, simply subtract the saved amount from the original price.

The Formula: Original Price - Saved Amount

  • 2500 - 750 = 1750

The final sale price of the shirt is Rs. 1,750.
