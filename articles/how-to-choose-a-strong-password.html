5 Simple Tips for Creating an Unbreakable Password

In our digital lives, a password is the key to our most sensitive information. A weak password is like leaving your front door unlocked. Here are five easy-to-remember tips to create strong passwords that keep your accounts safe.

1. Make it Long

Length is the single most important factor. A short password can be cracked in seconds. Aim for a password that is at least 12-15 characters long. The longer, the better.

2. Use a Mix of Characters

Don't just use letters. A strong password includes a combination of:

  • Uppercase letters (A-Z)
  • Lowercase letters (a-z)
  • Numbers (0-9)
  • Symbols (!, @, #, $, etc.)

3. Avoid Personal Information

Never use information that is easy to guess, such as your name, birthday, pet's name, or anniversary. Hackers can often find this information on your social media profiles.

4. Don't Use Common Words

Avoid dictionary words like "password," "iloveyou," or "qwerty." Hackers use programs that can try millions of common words per second. A better approach is to use a memorable but random phrase, like `Red-Car-Drives-Fast!7`.
