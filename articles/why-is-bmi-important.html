Why is BMI Important? A Simple Guide to Understanding Your Health Score

In the world of health and fitness, you often hear the term "BMI". But what exactly is it, and why does it matter? Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation using a person's height and weight. It's a key tool used to quickly gauge whether a person has a healthy body weight.

What Does Your BMI Score Mean?

BMI provides a general category for your weight status. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), the categories are:

  • Below 18.5: Underweight
  • 18.5 – 24.9: Normal or Healthy Weight
  • 25.0 – 29.9: Overweight
  • 30.0 and Above: Obesity

Knowing your category can be the first step towards a healthier lifestyle.

Limitations of BMI

It's important to remember that BMI is a general guide. It doesn't distinguish between fat and muscle. Therefore, very muscular people, like athletes, might have a high BMI but still be healthy. For most people, however, it's a very reliable indicator of body fatness.
